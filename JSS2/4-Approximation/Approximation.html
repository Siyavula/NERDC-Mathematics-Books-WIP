Rounding off: an introduction

Round the following number off to the hundredths place: 12.224212.2242.

Answer:

When a number has lots of decimal digits, we sometimes round off to simplify the number. Rounding off changes the number, but only by a small amount.

The first thing is to figure out which of the digits in the number tells us what to do: we always look at the digit one place further along than we are rounding the number to. In this question we are rounding off to the hundredths place - which is the second decimal place - so we must look at the value of the third decimal to decide what to do.

It is helpful to separate the number into two parts by drawing a line (|) between the second decimal and the third decimal, like this:

12.224212.22|4212.224212.22|42

If the number following the line is less than 55, we should round down; if it is greater than or equal to 55, we round up.

In this case, the number after the line is "44." The 44 tells us to round down, which means down from 12.224212.2242 to 12.220012.2200 (the number gets smaller - that is why it is called rounding "down.")

We write this as: 12.224212.2212.224212.22. The symbol "" means "approximately equal to."

The correct answer is: 12.2212.22.
